Demand Intelligence for a Global Retailer

A Fortune 500 retailer replaced spreadsheet-driven forecasting with an agentic demand-intelligence system spanning 800+ stores and 120,000+ SKUs — cutting inventory carrying cost by 35%, doubling the speed of replenishment decisions, and freeing more than $40M in trapped working capital.

At a glance

  • Client profile: Fortune 500 multi-format retailer — 800+ stores across 14 markets, 120,000+ active SKUs, ~$9B annual revenue.
  • Engagement: 12-week initial deployment, then scaled chain-wide over two quarters.
  • Platforms: ReBi Data Fabric (data unification), ReBi Agent Studio (autonomous forecasting agents), ReBi Insight Hub (planner-facing analytics).

The Challenge

The retailer's demand planning ran on a patchwork of spreadsheets and a legacy statistical forecasting tool that had not kept pace with the business. Planners refreshed forecasts on a 7-day cycle, by which point promotions, weather swings, and competitor moves had already shifted demand. The cost of that lag showed up across the balance sheet:

18%
of inventory was overstock — slow-moving or excess units tying up cash and shelf space.
9%
average stockout rate on high-velocity items, driving lost sales and substitution.
68%
forecast accuracy at SKU-store level — barely better than a naïve baseline.
~40 hrs
of planner time per week spent manually reconciling data instead of making decisions.

What We Built

ReBi AI delivered an end-to-end demand-intelligence layer that turns raw signals into store-level replenishment decisions without manual intervention.

  • Unified signal layer (ReBi Data Fabric). Connected 11 source systems — POS, e-commerce, ERP, supplier lead-time feeds, loyalty data, plus external weather and local-events data — into a single governed feature store processing 2.3 billion daily signals.
  • Agentic forecasting (ReBi Agent Studio). Autonomous agents generate and continuously re-score a forecast for every one of the ~96 million store-SKU-day combinations, flagging exceptions and proposing replenishment quantities that planners approve rather than build.
  • Planner cockpit (ReBi Insight Hub). A natural-language analytics surface lets category managers ask "why did the forecast for region 4 move?" and get an attributable answer in seconds.

The Results

Within the first full quarter of chain-wide operation, the system delivered measurable, audited improvements against the client's own pre-engagement baseline:

We went from arguing about whose spreadsheet was right to trusting one number — and acting on it the same day. The working capital we freed up paid for the program several times over in the first year.

— Chief Supply Chain Officer, Global Retail Enterprise (client name withheld under NDA)

Why It Worked

The difference was not a single model but the operating loop around it: governed data the business trusted, agents that produced decisions rather than dashboards, and a human-in-the-loop approval flow that earned planner adoption from week one. By the end of the rollout, planners were approving more than 92% of agent-proposed orders without modification — the clearest signal that the intelligence had become part of how the business runs.
